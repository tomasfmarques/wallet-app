# Wallet360 — Cold Market Feedback & Suggestions
_Saved 2026-06-10. Harsh, honest product critique vs the market._

## The market reality (it's brutal)
Personal finance apps are one of the most saturated, highest-churn categories
that exists.
- Consumer PFM apps run **3–5% monthly churn**.
- **71% of finance-app users churn within 90 days**; ~**90% of all apps**
  abandoned by day 30.
- The one outlier: **YNAB (~75% 12-month retention)** — wins on
  *methodology + paid commitment*, not features.

In **Portugal** the field is already occupied:
- **Revolut** (#1 finance app in PT, super-app), **MB WAY** (#2).
- Bank apps: **Moey!**, **ActivoBank**.
- PFMs: **Fintonic** (Iberian-focused, free, AI insights, strong PT/ES open
  banking), **BudgetBakers Wallet / Spendee** (SaltEdge, 5,000+ EU banks).
- Simple trackers topping PT charts: **Tarsi**, **Money Manager**.

## Where Wallet360 honestly stands (cold)
- **Bank-sync, budgeting, investment-tracking are table stakes** — incumbents
  do all three, free, with brand trust + native mobile apps you don't have.
- **Trust is the killer problem.** Nobody connects their bank to an unknown
  indie web app. Fintonic/Revolut have that for free.
- **It's web-only.** Portugal is mobile-first; competitors have native apps
  with widgets, push, Face ID.
- **It's sprawling** (budget + investments + multi-credit + watchlist +
  projections). Breadth is a weakness — jack-of-all-PFM loses to focused tools.
- **No moat, no wedge, no distribution, no monetization model.**

## Success rate (depends on the goal)
- **Broad B2C PFM product:** ~**1–2%**. Out-resourced, out-trusted. Don't.
- **Focused PT niche tool** (see wedge): ~**10–15%** for a small loyal base.
- **Personal tool / portfolio showcase:** ~**95% — already a success.**

## The wedge that could actually differentiate it
Stop being "another PFM." The **credit + investments engines together** answer
a question no global app answers and every Portuguese homeowner asks:
**"amortizar o crédito ou investir o dinheiro?"**
You already have the PT-specific mortgage simulator (Euribor/TAN/spread —
underserved by English apps) AND a portfolio projection engine. **Fuse them
into an "amortizar vs investir" recommendation.** Position the app as
**"gestão de crédito + finanças para portugueses,"** not a generic tracker.

## Harsh, prioritized add/change list
1. **Pick the wedge, cut the rest from the pitch.** Lead with PT credit +
   "amortizar vs investir." Everything else is supporting cast.
2. **Make it an installable PWA** (home-screen icon, push, offline). Removes
   the "it's just a website" objection. Cheap win.
3. **Time-to-value < 2 minutes.** First run = import a statement → instant
   dashboard. Long/manual onboarding = churn.
4. **Proactive insights, not just tables.** "Gastaste +32% em Restauração."
   "Amortizar 5k poupa 8.2k de juros." Automate; current screens are passive.
5. **Lean into privacy as the trust play.** Not SaltEdge harvesting data —
   "os teus dados são teus, processados no teu browser."
6. **Don't commercialize without the legal layer.** Others connecting banks =
   you're a GDPR data controller (privacy policy, ToS, DPA, GoCardless AISP
   reliance). Personal use fine; public = lawyer first.
7. **Kill feature creep.** Before adding anything: "does this serve the
   credit/invest wedge?" If no, don't build it.

## Bottom line
As a **personal tool it's already a win.** As a **product**, it only has a shot
if you stop competing with Revolut on breadth and **own the Portuguese
credit-vs-invest corner nobody else does.**
